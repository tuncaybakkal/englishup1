Grammar Spot
Grammar Spot Answers
1
verb + infinitive (with to): want to work, hope to get, plan to do
verb + infinitive (without to): can leave, must stop
verb + -ing: enjoy doing, hate owing, love going
prepositions of, with, to + -ing: thinking of going, fed up with staying, looking forward to returning
2 "I like working in New York." → refers to NOW (present habit). "I'd like to work in New York." → HYPOTHETICAL / future wish.
3 Complete with "work in New York": I hope to work / I'd love to work / I'm thinking of working / I enjoy working / I'm looking forward to working / I'm fed up with working / I can't work in New York.

Everyday English
Expressing Doubt and Certainty
(Ders Kitabı s. 57 / Cevap Anahtarı s. 6–7)
Exercise 1 – Which phrases agree 100%? Disagree 100%? Not certain?
Agree 100%:
Of course he will. Yes, absolutely. Definitely!
Disagree 100%:
No chance. Definitely not. Not a chance.
Not certain:
He might do. Mmm … maybe. I doubt it. I think so. I'm not sure. I don't think so. Perhaps. They might do. Anything's possible.
