Grammar Spot
Grammar Spot Answers
1 Past Simple sentences (1, 3, 5): wrote, wore, married — used because the people are dead or the action happened at a specific finished time.
Present Perfect sentences (2, 4, 6): has been, has sung, has written — used because the people are still alive.
We form the Present Perfect with have/has + past participle.
2 Past Simple is used in "Steve Irwin made" because he is dead.
Present Perfect is used in "Bindi Irwin has made" because she is still alive.
❌ "John Lennon has played with The Beatles for 12 years" is wrong because he is dead — the sentence implies he is still alive.
3 We use for for a time period: for two hours / six weeks / ten years / a long time.
We use since for a point in time: since eight o'clock / yesterday / last week / 2010 / she was two.

Grammar Spot – Present Perfect vs Past Simple
Grammar Spot Answers
1
"You've travelled a lot in your lifetime." → Present Perfect – refers to an experience some time in your life.
"Which countries have you been to?" → Present Perfect – refers to an experience some time in your life.
"When I was six, my family moved to England." → Past Simple – refers to a definite time in the past.
2 The adverbs always, ever, and never are often used with the Present Perfect:
• I've always been interested in archaeology.
• Have you ever been in danger?
• I've never been to South America.

Exercise 2 – How are question tags formed? (6.16)
Question Tag Formation Rules
• The question tag uses the same verb as the main sentence (auxiliary or modal verb).
• We use do/does in Present Simple tags, and did in Past Simple tags.
• If the main clause is positive → the tag is negative.
If the main clause is negative → the tag is positive.
Examples from text: isn't it? · don't we? · didn't we? · was it? · won't she? · doesn't she? · hasn't she?

I come from Newcastle in England, but I live in Perth, Australia, now. I've been here nearly ten years. My wife, Jodie, is Australian, and our children, Russell and Alice, were born here.
