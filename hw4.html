s.41Grammar – Röportaj Tamamlama (Alıştırma 3)
Abbie ile röportaj – boşlukları doldurun
1 I:How much baby food do you eat in a day?
A: Quite a lot! About 500 spoonfuls. But they're small spoons!
2 I:How many taste buds do you have?
A: A lot! About 10–15,000 – the same as most babies.
3 I:At home, do you eat a lot of vegetables?
A: Yes, I love peas! But I only eat a little broccoli and spinach, and I can only eat a few sprouts.
4 I:Do you drink any coffee?
A: No, I drink tea, and lots of water to clean my taste buds.
5 I:And do you drink much milk?
A: I can only drink a little milk – it tastes like cream to me!
6 I:Did you eat many sweets as a child?
A: No. I don't like many sweet things, but I enjoy a little chocolate, if there isn't much sugar in it.
7 I:Do you use any hot spices in your cooking?
A: No, I don't, and obviously, I don't eat any chillies – they're too hot for a lot of people!
8 I:And do you drink any alcohol?
A: Yes, I drink some. I don't drink any red wine, but I have a glass of white wine sometimes. But definitely no beer! Bleurgh!
s.41Grammar Spot – Miktar İfadeleri
1 – Hangi grup hangisiyle kullanılır?
A (How much / not much / a little) → Uncountable (Sayılamaz isimler)
B (How many / not many / a few) → Countable Plural (Sayılabilir çoğul)
C (some/any / not any/no / a lot of/lots of) → Both (Her ikisi)
2 – Much/many olumlu cümlelerde
There are many books in my bag. / There's much homework tonight. — Düzeltme?
There aren't many books in my bag. / There are a lot of books in my bag.
There isn't much homework tonight. / There's a lot of homework tonight.
3 – "some" ile "any" farkı
Which is a request? "Do we have any orange juice?" / "Can I have some orange juice?"
"Can I have some orange juice?" = a request (rica).
some → olumlu cümleler + ricalar · any → sorular + olumsuzlar

Grammar Spot – Makale Kuralları (Alıştırma 3)
İlk kez bahsedilen (first reference) → a/an
a shopkeeper · a fish and chip shop · an old village · a big industrial town · a son · a daughter · a happy and contented man · a long life · a glass of whisky
Konuşmacı ve dinleyicinin zaten bildiği → the
the daughter · the shop · the village · the local pub
Tek olan şeyler → the
the North · the whole country · the area · the secret
Üstünlük sıfatlarıyla → the
the most popular dish · the best fish and chips
Miktar ifadesiyle → a
once a week
Genel anlamda (çoğul / sayılamaz) → article yok (–)
fish and chips · by bus · to bed · have lunch

s.44–45Reading – Recipes for Success (Delicious Accidents!)
Group A – Ice Lollies (Popsicles)
1What are they called in the US and in the UK?
Popsicles in the US, ice lollies in the UK.
2Who invented them? When and where?
Frank Epperson, in 1905 in San Francisco.
3How did the accident happen?
He left a drink made with water and flavoured powder outside, with a stick in it – it froze overnight.
4Who were the first people to try the result?
Frank's friends.
5What did they think of them?
They liked them.
6Did the person make a lot of money?
No, because he sold the patent. The Popsicles company is now successful as a result.
7How many are sold each year in the US?
2 billion per year.
Group B – Crisps (Potato Chips)
1What are they called in the US and in the UK?
Potato chips in the US, crisps in the UK.
2Who invented them? When and where?
George Crum, 1853, Saratoga Springs in New York State.
3How did the accident happen?
A customer complained the fried potatoes were too thick; Crum made them thinner and thinner until they were hard and crisp.
4–5Who were the first to try them? What did they think?
The complaining customer – he loved them.
6–7Did he make money? Which company is successful?
No – he never got a patent. Lays is now successful. Over $1.5 billion worth of crisps every year.
Group C – Chocolate Chip Cookies (Biscuits)
1What are they called in the US and in the UK?
Cookies in the US, biscuits in the UK.
2Who invented them? When and where?
Mary Wakefield, in the 1930s, in Massachusetts, US.
3How did the accident happen?
She had no baking chocolate, used Nestlé's chocolate instead – it didn't melt, leaving pieces in the cookies.
4–5Who were the first to try them? What did they think?
Mary and her hotel guests – they thought they were delicious.
6–7Did she make money?
No – but she received a lifetime's supply of chocolate. Nestlé made a lot of money. 3.5 billion sold per year in the US.
Alıştırma 5 – a/an, the, –, some veya any ile tamamlayın
1 (Ice Lollies)
Frank left a drink outside with – stick in it. In the morning, the drink was frozen with the stick in the middle of it. It was an ice lolly!
2 (Crisps)
A customer in – George's restaurant ordered some French fries. He sent some fries back and asked for – thinner ones.
3 (Chocolate Chip Cookies)
Mary wanted to make some chocolate cookies, but she didn't have any baking chocolate. The chocolate she used didn't melt, and the result was – chocolate chip cookies.
